What makes it different

¶

It has a constitution

DARA.md — 15 writing rules. The first file any AI reads. No training needed. The rules ARE the onboarding.

↵

It compiles

A 10-step pipeline: validates, deduplicates, auto-fixes, compresses, checksums. Nobody else compiles their memory.

≡

Rules control, not roles

Claude, GPT, DeepSeek, n8n — anyone writes if they follow the protocol. The constitution is the access control.

⊕

It self-heals

Errors are expected. The next AI that spots one fixes it. Consensus flags protect against unilateral changes.

∅

Zero infrastructure

Markdown + Python stdlib. No database. No Docker. No API. Runs anywhere Python 3.10+ exists.

Share a neuron with your team

Drop a .md file in Drive. Your colleague opens it with Claude, GPT, or DeepSeek. No install needed. It's just text — every AI understands it.

Agents work the same way. agent-librarian.md contains the full librarian protocol. Any AI that reads it becomes the librarian. The intelligence is in the file, not in who runs it.
